# Why I'm Building a Reader That Doesn't Summarize

Everyone says they're reading more than ever. By which they mean: listening to 30-minute book summaries on their commute. Asking ChatGPT to explain Foucault in three paragraphs. Reading the TLDR.

They are not reading. They are consuming reading-shaped content.

## The bottleneck moved, and we didn't notice

For most of human history, the bottleneck on knowledge was access. We fixed that. Then it was attention — Readwise, Matter, Reader, Pocket are still fixing that.

Now AI is solving something nobody asked it to solve: it's solving comprehension.

It's solving comprehension by handing you the answer.

## What summaries actually take from you

When you read something hard, your mind does work. It resists. It asks: do I believe this? It cross-references against what you already know. It generates objections. It gets confused, then slowly finds clarity. That process — the friction, the struggle, the slow reorganization — is what makes reading change you.

Summaries give you the conclusion without the journey. And without the journey, you don't change. You collect facts you'll forget in a week. You get the satisfying feeling of *"I learned something"* without the actual learning.

A summary tells you what someone else thought. Reading lets you discover what *you* think.

## The new value of reading

In 2026, reading is no longer about information. ChatGPT has any fact. Perplexity will synthesize any debate. If you're still reading for information, you're reading wrong.

Reading now is about *transformation*. The slow construction of your own judgment. The accumulation of perspective. The reshaping of what kind of person you are. None of these can be downloaded.

But all of them require friction. And friction is exactly what every consumer AI tool is racing to eliminate.

## What Skimless does

Skimless is an AI reader that doesn't summarize.

Give it a long article, a paper, a chapter. It won't tell you the takeaways. It asks you five layered questions.

The first asks what you noticed. The next asks why the author framed it that way. The next probes a contradiction. The next asks what would change your mind. The last asks what you'll do differently.

You don't read faster. You read deeper. Your answers stay — searchable, exportable, yours. Over time, you build something most readers never had: **a library not of what you've read, but of what you've actually thought.**

It's the opposite of every AI reading product on the market. That's the point.

## Who this is for

Skimless isn't for everyone. It's for people who already know the goal was never to read more books. The people who use Anki and Readwise and journal their notes — not because they have to, but because they refuse to outsource their own mind.

The researcher who'd rather understand one paper than skim ten. The investor who knows consensus is the enemy. The writer who reads to think, not to perform.

If you're optimizing your reading life to consume more, you'll hate Skimless. If you've quietly suspected that all this optimization is making you less, not more — you're who I'm building for.
